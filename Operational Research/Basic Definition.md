Operations Research is the application of **mathematical models, statistical analysis, and optimization techniques** to aid in **decision-making, planning, and problem-solving** in complex systems.
1. GOALS: -
	To **maximize or minimize** some objective — such as:
	 - Maximize **profit, efficiency, productivity**
	 - Minimize **cost, time, risk, or waste**
2.  KEY STEPS: -
		**Define the problem** clearly
	    **Construct a mathematical model** of the system
	    **Derive solutions** using analytical or computational methods
	    **Test and validate** the model with real data
	    **Implement and monitor** the solution in the real system
3. COMMON TECHNIQUES USED: 
	- **Linear Programming (LP)** – for optimizing linear systems
	 - **Integer Programming (IP)** – when decisions are discrete (e.g., yes/no)
	- **Dynamic Programming (DP)** – for multistage problems
	- **Simulation** – for systems too complex to model exactly
	- **Queuing Theory** – for analysing waiting lines or service systems
	- **Inventory Control Models** – for managing stock efficiently
	- **Game Theory** – for strategic decision-making among competitors
	- **Network Models** – for transportation, logistics, or communication problems
